The **Usage** tab in Console shows how your app is being used over time. This is also where your billing comes from — every plan has a usage allowance, and overage is billed at the rates listed in [Pricing](/pricing).

## What each metric means

- **API calls** — every successful HTTP request your app makes to `api.oxy.so`. 4xx errors don't count.
- **Auth requests** — sign-in attempts that flow through your app's OAuth client. Counted once per user per day, not once per token refresh.
- **Storage** — total bytes occupied by files your users have uploaded through your app.
- **Bandwidth** — bytes downloaded by users from your app's storage.

## Reading the chart

The default view is the last 30 days, bucketed by day. Switch to **Hour** for a 24-hour view or **Month** to see your billing trajectory.

<Callout type="tip" title="Set usage alerts">
  In **Usage → Alerts** you can set a threshold (e.g. 80% of plan) — we email you when you cross it. No surprise overages.
</Callout>

## When numbers look off

If a metric looks wrong, check:

1. Are you on the right environment? Dev and prod have separate counters.
2. Are some requests authenticated as a different app? Check the **Top callers** breakdown.
3. Is there a [status incident](/status) that might be artificially inflating retries?

Still confused — open a support ticket from the **?** menu and we'll dig in.
