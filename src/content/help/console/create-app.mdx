A **developer app** is an OAuth2 client registration. Every app you build that calls Oxy APIs on behalf of users — or makes server-side requests — needs one.

## Create an app

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Console">
    Sign in at [console.oxy.so](https://console.oxy.so).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap New app">
    Choose a name (visible to users) and a redirect URI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the scopes you need">
    Start small — `profile:read` and `email:send` are the most common. Users see the scope list during OAuth consent.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you get

Console gives you two credentials:

- **API key** (`oxy_dk_…`) — public-ish, included in the OAuth flow.
- **API secret** (`oxy_sk_…`) — server-side only, never ship to a browser or mobile app.

<Callout type="warning" title="Treat your secret like a password">
  Anyone with your API secret can impersonate your app. Rotate it from **App → Credentials → Rotate** if it leaks.
</Callout>

## Production checklist

- [ ] Real redirect URIs (no `localhost` in prod)
- [ ] Privacy policy and terms URLs filled in (we surface them in the consent screen)
- [ ] Webhook endpoint added (for billing/usage events)
- [ ] App icon uploaded (shown in the consent screen)
