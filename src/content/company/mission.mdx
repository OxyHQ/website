We build ethical technology that solves real problems, giving people the tools to shape their own futures, not just consume someone else's vision.

## North star

A world where technology creates opportunity for everyone, not just those who can afford it.

## How we get there

The mission is big. The work is incremental — one product, one feature, one user at a time.

### Build for the long term

We ignore quarterly milestones in favor of decade-long impact. Boring software that lasts decades beats hype software that lasts two years. Every architectural decision is made with the assumption that someone will be running this code in 2050.

### Empower individuals

Every product we ship aims to give people more control over their digital lives — not less. More understanding of how systems work. More autonomy over their data. More ability to change things they don't like.

### Stay accessible

Software that only the wealthy can afford is software that recreates the inequalities of the offline world. We design for accessibility — financial, technical, physical, geographic. Most of what we build will always be free.

### Operate transparently

We publish how we make money, where we spend it, what mistakes we've made, and what we're working on next. There are no information asymmetries between us and the people we serve.

## Where we won't compromise

These are the lines we will not cross, regardless of business pressure:

- **No surveillance.** We will not build tools that track users for advertising or profiling.
- **No closed source.** Every product we ship is open. If we cannot open it, we don't ship it.
- **No data sales.** Your information is not a product line.
- **No dark patterns.** No manipulative defaults, no buried opt-outs, no manufactured FOMO.

These aren't slogans. They're the operating constraints that produce everything we make.
