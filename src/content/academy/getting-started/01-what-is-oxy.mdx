Oxy is a network of small, open-source apps that share one portable identity. Instead of having a different account for every service in your digital life — email, social, file storage, developer tools — you have **one Oxy ID** that unlocks all of them.

## The problem we're solving

Every app you use today asks you to make a new account, hand over your data, and trust them to protect it. Most of them don't. The model is broken.

Oxy flips that:

- **One account, many apps.** Your identity is portable.
- **You hold the keys.** Encryption keys live on your device, not on our servers.
- **Everything is open.** Audit it, fork it, self-host it.

<Callout type="info" title="Open source from day one">
  Every line of Oxy code is on [GitHub](https://github.com/OxyHQ) under an OSI-approved license.
</Callout>

## What's in the ecosystem

Right now Oxy includes:

- **Accounts** — manage your Oxy ID
- **Inbox** — end-to-end encrypted email
- **Mention** — social posting that federates with the wider fediverse
- **Console** — developer dashboard
- **Allo** — encrypted real-time messaging
- **Homiio** — collaborative file management

More products are being built every quarter. Because everything shares the same identity, they all just work together — sign in once, you're in everywhere.

## Why now

The last decade taught us that "free" services that survey you for ad targeting produce hostile products. We can build software that respects people instead — and still pay the bills honestly. Oxy is what that looks like.
