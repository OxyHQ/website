You have an Oxy ID. Now let's actually use it. We'll open Inbox — Oxy's encrypted email app — as a worked example, but the same flow applies to every Oxy app.

## Open Inbox

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit inbox.oxy.so">
    Or open the Inbox app on your device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in via FedCM">
    The browser's account picker pops up. You'll see your Oxy ID listed. Tap **Continue as [your name]**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the key restore">
    Inbox restores your private key from secure device storage. This takes about a second the first time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You're in">
    The inbox view loads. If you have an existing email address you want to bring with you, tap **Connect external email** and walk through the IMAP setup wizard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Try sending an end-to-end encrypted message

Send a test message to yourself (`yourusername@oxy.so`) so you can see what an encrypted message looks like in transit. The lock icon next to the subject means the body was encrypted on your device.

<Callout type="tip" title="The same flow works everywhere">
  Try Mention next — sign in once, scroll through the federated feed, post your first toot. No new account, no new password.
</Callout>

## What you learned

You now have:

1. A working Oxy ID
2. A signed-in Inbox session
3. A safe recovery phrase
4. A mental model of how cross-app sign-in works

That's the whole onboarding. From here, every Oxy app is a single tap away.
