Bad things happen. Phones get lost. Laptops die. People forget passwords. Recovery is the safety net you set up *before* any of that occurs.

## Recovery scenarios

There are five things that can go wrong, in increasing order of severity:

1. **Forgot your password** — easy. Reset it via your recovery email.
2. **Lost a device** — easy. Sign in on another device, revoke the lost one.
3. **Lost the only device you had** — moderate. Use your recovery phrase on a new device.
4. **Lost the only device AND the recovery phrase** — hard. You can still get back in if you have backup codes for your 2FA — but you'll lose access to encrypted data on the device.
5. **Lost everything** — game over. There is no master backdoor. This is the trade-off for true end-to-end encryption.

## Recovery flows

### Forgotten password

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap Forgot password on the sign-in screen">
    A reset link is sent to your recovery email.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the link">
    Choose a new password. The new password takes effect immediately and invalidates all existing sessions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Lost device

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in on any other device you trust">
    The Devices screen lists all active sessions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Revoke on the lost device">
    Optionally mark it as lost or stolen to trigger automatic key rotation for encrypted content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Lost all devices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get a new device">
    Any platform Oxy runs on works.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Accounts and tap I have a recovery phrase">
    On the welcome screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your 24-word recovery phrase">
    In order. Spaces are required. The app re-derives your private key from the phrase.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in normally">
    Your identity is restored. End-to-end encrypted data from before this point may not be readable if it was sealed with a per-device session key — see below.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What's recoverable, what isn't

| Data | Recoverable from phrase? |
|------|--------------------------|
| Your account / username | Yes |
| Profile info (avatar, color, bio) | Yes |
| Public posts in Mention | Yes |
| Plain-text email metadata in Inbox | Yes |
| End-to-end encrypted Inbox messages stored only on a lost device | No |
| Allo chat history stored only on a lost device | No |

This is why Oxy backs up encrypted-message blobs to your other devices — so losing one doesn't lose conversation history. If you only had one device, lose that device, and never wrote down your phrase, the encrypted content is gone.

<Callout type="danger" title="Test your recovery">
  Once a year, do a recovery drill: install Accounts on a spare device, restore from your phrase, verify everything works, then wipe the spare. Don't wait until an emergency to find out your written phrase has a typo.
</Callout>
