Your private key is a secret. The Accounts app stores it for you, but you should know where it lives, what protects it, and what to do when something goes wrong.

## Where the key lives

Depending on your device:

- **iOS / iPadOS** — in the Secure Enclave (hardware-protected). The key never leaves the chip.
- **Android** — in the Hardware-backed Keystore where available, fallback to the StrongBox or software keystore.
- **macOS** — in the Keychain.
- **Windows** — in the Credential Manager.
- **Linux** — in libsecret (Gnome Keyring, KWallet).
- **Browser (web)** — in `IndexedDB` with the WebCrypto-backed encryption key in a non-extractable form.

In every case, the operating system protects the key from other apps reading it.

## Adding a new device

When you sign in on a new device, that device needs your key too. You have two options:

### Option A — paste your recovery phrase

The safest if you have your 24-word phrase handy. The new device re-derives your key from the phrase. The phrase is never transmitted.

### Option B — scan a QR code from an existing device

Faster. On your existing device: **Accounts → Devices → Add device**. A QR code appears, expiring in 60 seconds. Scan it with the new device's camera. The keys are transferred over an end-to-end encrypted channel.

<Callout type="warning" title="Never share your QR code">
  The Add Device QR is a one-time, time-limited token but it still transfers your identity. Don't put it on a screenshare. Don't post it.
</Callout>

## Removing a device

If you sell a device or it gets stolen, revoke it from **Accounts → Devices → Revoke**. The revocation is signed by your master key and broadcast to every other device. The revoked device's session becomes invalid immediately.

After revocation, that device can't decrypt new messages — but it can still read messages it already decrypted (because those were stored locally on the device). This is why marking a device as **lost/stolen** also triggers a key-rotation: new messages use a new key the old device doesn't have.

## Backing up

The recovery phrase IS the backup. There's no separate "export key" file. If you have your phrase written down and stored securely, you have a full backup.
